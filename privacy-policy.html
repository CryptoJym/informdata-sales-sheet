Privacy Policy

Effective Date: January 1, 2025 | Last Updated: January 1, 2025

Important Notice

Vuplicity is a Consumer Reporting Agency (CRA) regulated under the Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA). This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, and protect your personal information when you use our background screening services.

1. Information We Collect

1.1 Information from Employers

When an employer or client engages our services, we collect:

  • Company name, address, and contact information
  • Authorized user account information
  • Payment and billing information
  • Usage data and service interaction logs

1.2 Information about Candidates/Consumers

With proper authorization, we collect information necessary to conduct background checks, including:

  • Full name, date of birth, Social Security number
  • Current and previous addresses
  • Employment history and educational background
  • Criminal records, driving records, and credit history (when applicable)
  • Professional licenses and certifications
  • Reference information

1.3 Information from Third-Party Sources

We obtain consumer information from various sources including:

  • Public records (courts, DMVs, corrections facilities)
  • Credit bureaus and consumer reporting agencies
  • Educational institutions and licensing boards
  • Previous employers and professional references
  • Government databases and watchlists

2. How We Use Your Information

2.1 For Employers/Clients

  • Provide background screening and verification services
  • Process payments and maintain billing records
  • Communicate about services and support requests
  • Improve our services and develop new features
  • Comply with legal and regulatory obligations

2.2 For Candidates/Consumers

We process consumer information solely for permissible purposes under the FCRA, including:

  • Employment screening and verification
  • Providing consumer reports to authorized users
  • Responding to consumer inquiries and dispute requests
  • Complying with legal requirements and court orders

3. Information Sharing and Disclosure

We do not sell personal information. We share information only in the following circumstances:

3.1 With Authorized Employers/Clients

Consumer reports are provided only to clients with a permissible purpose under FCRA, and only after obtaining proper written authorization from the consumer.

3.2 With Service Providers

We work with trusted third-party service providers who assist in:

  • Data verification and information retrieval (e.g., InformData)
  • Payment processing and billing
  • Technology infrastructure and hosting
  • Customer support and communications

All service providers are contractually bound to protect your information and use it only for authorized purposes.

3.3 For Legal Compliance

We may disclose information when required by law, including:

  • In response to subpoenas, court orders, or legal processes
  • To comply with government investigations or regulatory requests
  • To protect our rights, property, or safety
  • In connection with fraud prevention or security incidents

4. Consumer Rights Under FCRA

As a consumer subject to a background check, you have specific rights:

4.1 Right to Disclosure

You must receive a clear disclosure that a background check may be conducted, provided on a standalone document.

4.2 Right to Authorization

Your written authorization is required before we conduct any background screening.

4.3 Right to Access Your File

You may request a copy of your consumer file at any time. Contact us at privacy@vuplicity.com to request your file disclosure.

4.4 Right to Dispute

If you believe information in your report is inaccurate or incomplete, you have the right to dispute it. We will investigate and correct any verified errors within 30 days.

4.5 Right to Adverse Action Notice

If an employer takes adverse action based on your background report, they must provide you with:

  • A copy of the consumer report
  • A Summary of Your Rights Under the FCRA
  • Our contact information

5. Data Security and Retention

5.1 Security Measures

We implement industry-standard security measures to protect your information:

  • SOC 2 Type II certified infrastructure
  • Encryption of data in transit (TLS 1.2+) and at rest (AES-256)
  • Multi-factor authentication for user accounts
  • Regular security audits and penetration testing
  • Access controls and employee background screening
  • Continuous monitoring and incident response procedures

5.2 Data Retention

We retain consumer information in accordance with FCRA requirements:

  • Consumer reports: 7 years from date of creation
  • Dispute records: 5 years from resolution
  • Authorization forms: Duration of relationship plus 5 years
  • Billing records: 7 years as required by law

6. Cookies and Tracking Technologies

Our website uses cookies and similar technologies to:

  • Maintain user sessions and authentication
  • Remember user preferences
  • Analyze site usage and improve performance
  • Provide security and fraud protection

You can control cookie settings through your browser. However, disabling certain cookies may limit functionality. See our Cookie Policy for details.

7. California Privacy Rights (CCPA/CPRA)

California residents have additional rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act:

  • Right to Know: Request information about personal data we collect and share
  • Right to Delete: Request deletion of personal information (subject to FCRA retention requirements)
  • Right to Opt-Out: We do not sell personal information
  • Right to Non-Discrimination: We will not discriminate for exercising your rights

To exercise these rights, contact privacy@vuplicity.com or call 1-800-XXX-XXXX.

8. Children's Privacy

Our services are not directed to individuals under 18 years of age. We do not knowingly collect information from minors. If we discover we have inadvertently collected such information, we will promptly delete it.

9. International Data Transfers

Our services are primarily provided within the United States. If you access our services from outside the U.S., your information may be transferred to and processed in the United States, where privacy laws may differ from those in your jurisdiction.

10. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy periodically to reflect changes in our practices or legal requirements. We will notify you of material changes by:

  • Posting the updated policy on our website
  • Updating the "Last Updated" date
  • Sending email notification for significant changes
