What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files stored on your device when you visit a website. They help websites remember your preferences, maintain your session, and provide analytics to improve user experience.

1. How We Use Cookies

Vuplicity uses cookies and similar tracking technologies to provide and improve our background screening services. We are committed to transparency about our data practices and your privacy choices.

2. Types of Cookies We Use

2.1 Strictly Necessary Cookies

These cookies are essential for the website to function properly and cannot be disabled in our systems.

Session Cookies

  • User authentication and login state
  • Form data persistence during multi-step processes
  • Session security and CSRF protection

Duration: Session (deleted when browser closes)

Security Cookies

  • Fraud detection and prevention
  • Rate limiting and abuse protection
  • Two-factor authentication state

Duration: Session to 24 hours

2.2 Functional Cookies

These cookies enable enhanced functionality and personalization. They may be set by us or third-party providers.

Cookie NamePurposeDuration
user_preferences Stores your UI preferences (theme, dashboard layout) 1 year
language Remembers your language preference 1 year
notification_settings Stores notification preferences 6 months

2.3 Analytics and Performance Cookies

These cookies help us understand how visitors interact with our website, allowing us to improve performance and user experience.

  • Google Analytics: Tracks page views, session duration, bounce rate, and user flows
  • Performance Monitoring: Identifies slow pages, errors, and technical issues
  • Feature Usage: Measures which features are most/least used

These cookies do not collect personally identifiable information without your consent. Analytics data is aggregated and anonymized.

2.4 Marketing and Advertising Cookies (Optional)

With your consent, we may use cookies to deliver relevant advertisements and measure campaign effectiveness:

  • Tracking conversion from marketing campaigns
  • Retargeting ads on third-party platforms
  • A/B testing for marketing content

Note: We do not use marketing cookies without explicit consent. You can manage these preferences in your account settings.

4. Local Storage and Similar Technologies

In addition to cookies, we use other browser storage mechanisms:

4.1 Local Storage

  • Purpose: Store larger amounts of data locally (e.g., cached search results, draft forms)
  • Duration: Persistent until manually cleared
  • Data Type: Non-sensitive application state and preferences

4.2 Session Storage

  • Purpose: Temporary storage for active session data
  • Duration: Cleared when tab/window is closed
  • Data Type: Form data, navigation state

4.3 IndexedDB

  • Purpose: Store structured application data for offline functionality
  • Duration: Persistent until manually cleared
  • Data Type: Cached API responses, offline work queue

5. Your Cookie Choices

5.1 Browser Controls

Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings. You can:

  • Block all cookies
  • Accept only first-party cookies
  • Delete cookies after each session
  • Receive notifications when cookies are set

Browser-Specific Instructions:

  • Chrome: Settings → Privacy and Security → Cookies and other site data
  • Firefox: Settings → Privacy & Security → Cookies and Site Data
  • Safari: Preferences → Privacy → Cookies and website data
  • Edge: Settings → Cookies and site permissions → Manage and delete cookies

5.2 Opt-Out Links

You can opt out of specific third-party cookies:

  • Google Analytics: Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on
  • Advertising Cookies: NAI Opt-Out Tool
  • Do Not Track (DNT): We honor DNT browser signals

5.3 Vuplicity Account Settings

Logged-in users can manage cookie preferences in their account settings:

  1. Navigate to Account Settings → Privacy & Cookies
  2. Toggle cookie categories on/off (except strictly necessary cookies)
  3. Save your preferences

5.4 Impact of Disabling Cookies

Important Notice

Blocking or deleting cookies may impact your experience on Vuplicity:

  • You may need to log in repeatedly
  • Your preferences will not be saved
  • Some features may not function properly
  • Multi-step forms may not work correctly

Strictly necessary cookies cannot be disabled as they are essential for service functionality and security.

6. Mobile Applications

The Vuplicity mobile application uses similar tracking technologies:

  • Device Identifiers: Anonymous device IDs for analytics and session management
  • SDK Technologies: Third-party SDKs (analytics, crash reporting) may collect usage data
  • Push Notifications: Device tokens for sending notifications (with your permission)

You can manage mobile app tracking in your device settings:

  • iOS: Settings → Privacy → Tracking
  • Android: Settings → Google → Ads → Opt out of Ads Personalization
